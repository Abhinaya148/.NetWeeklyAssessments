Food festivals are thought to be strengthening agents for local cultural heritage because they both commemorate and commercialize it for a larger national or worldwide audience. While historically associated with culturally significant food harvesting periods, contemporary food festivals are typically associated with business entities or nonprofit organizations and engage in extensive marketing for their festivals, as their success is measured by the amount of revenue they generate for the local community, region, or entity putting on the event.
